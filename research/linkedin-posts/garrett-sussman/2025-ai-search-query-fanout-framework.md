## Content
Sussman opens by defending AI search query research against critics who dismiss it as too messy/imprecise to be useful. His argument: traditional SEO relied on short head-term keyword volume, but AI search behavior is shifting toward full conversational questions, and "query fan-out" (where a single query spins off many related sub-queries) means those old head-term metrics are losing functional value — though tools like Profound are starting to make fan-out data usable. He then pivots to a practical, low-cost checklist for optimizing for AI search visibility in 2026, starting with a basic technical check: verify robots.txt isn't blocking AI crawlers (GPTBot, Claude-Web, CCBot, etc.), framing this as brands unknowingly telling AI systems to ignore them entirely.
