## Content
Simmonds names a phenomenon he calls "the Citation Gap": a brand can be well-represented in AI answers ("a star for ChatGPT") while getting zero click-throughs to its site, because AI engines behave differently by platform. He characterizes ChatGPT as a "storyteller" — only about 12% of its answers include links, because it wants to be the endpoint of the conversation rather than a middleman — versus Perplexity as a "librarian," where roughly 95% of answers include links, making it the more reliable traffic driver of the two. His practical advice for closing the gap: publish original data, fresh statistics, and unique case studies (AI tends to link out when the specific data point is too specific to simply restate); use tables, lists, and clearly labeled headings; and write two versions of the same content — a short summary for ChatGPT-style synthesis, and a fuller stats-and-numbers version for platforms like Perplexity/Gemini that are more likely to convert a citation into a click. He also cites a stat that roughly 70% of brand mentions in AI chat are effectively "dark" — invisible in standard analytics.

## Why this post matters for the playbook
Gives a platform-specific, actionable framework (not just "get cited," but "get cited somewhere that actually links") that's more tactical than his existing two collected posts in this repo.
