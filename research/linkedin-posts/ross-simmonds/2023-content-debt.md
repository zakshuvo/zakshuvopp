## Content

Simmonds coins "content debt" as the content-marketing equivalent of technical debt: posts from years earlier that ranked well but were left to rot while teams kept chasing the "create more content" mantra, accumulating outdated references, weak visuals, and broken external links. He describes his agency, Foundation Marketing, running a project to update a client's backlog of old blog posts rather than only producing new ones, framing this unglamorous maintenance work as what actually protects search rankings long-term.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Reinforces (independently of Ryan Law's Ahrefs automation post) that AI-era content operations increasingly treat refreshing/maintaining existing content as equally important to new production — a recurring theme worth calling out explicitly in the playbook rather than treating "content production" as only meaning new drafts.
