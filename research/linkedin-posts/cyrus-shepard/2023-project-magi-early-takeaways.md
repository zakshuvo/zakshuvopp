## Content

Shepard lists early observations from Google's then-unreleased AI search experience (internally "Project Magi"): most AI-generated answers didn't cite sources directly; supporting links appeared off to the side rather than clearly marked as sources; shopping features were emphasized; search ads still appeared; and, similar to ChatGPT, users could continue a conversation rather than issuing one-off queries. He notes most resulting clicks likely stayed within other Google properties (Maps, YouTube, Images) rather than going to independent websites.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

An early, concrete forecast of what became AI Overviews/AI Mode — useful as a historical marker for how the traffic-and-citation dynamics practitioners are still discussing in 2025-2026 (see his more recent "AI citation snake oil" commentary, which argues concentrated traffic patterns predate AI search and citation-chasing may not translate to real business value) were anticipated well before rollout.
