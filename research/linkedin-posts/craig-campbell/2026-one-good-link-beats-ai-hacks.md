## Summary (paraphrased)

Campbell shares a brief case example: a page that had been stuck/buried in
the rankings jumped to page 2 after his team built a single high-quality,
relevant "niche edit" style link. His point is explicitly a counter to the
idea that AI tools or hacks are needed to move stuck pages — he attributes
the result to relevance, quality, and basic link-building strategy rather
than any AI-driven shortcut, and argues that most businesses (as opposed to
large corporate brands with existing authority) still see real ranking
movement from acquiring good links. He frames a stalled page as more likely
an authority problem than a content problem.
