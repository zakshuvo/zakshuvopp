## Content

Campbell cites 2026 consumer-behavior data showing a two-step search pattern: roughly a third of consumers now start product/topic discovery in an LLM like ChatGPT or Perplexity, but the large majority of those users still go on to Google afterward to fact-check or dig deeper. He argues traditional search remains huge in absolute query volume, so the practical implication isn't "replace SEO with AI visibility" but "track both" — brands need to show up at the start of the journey (in AI answers) and at the end (in traditional search results), since optimizing for only one leaves a gap at the other stage.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Offers a specific behavioral data point (cited stats on LLM-first discovery followed by Google verification) supporting a "total presence across AI and traditional search" strategy — useful as a counterweight to takes that frame AI search and traditional SEO as an either/or tradeoff.
