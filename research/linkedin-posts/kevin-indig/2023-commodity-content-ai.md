## Content

Indig argues commodity content is a losing bet once AI drives the marginal cost of producing it toward zero. His framing: marketers either get technical enough to feed AI proprietary data so the output becomes genuinely unique, or they skip that arms race and compete on differentiation instead. He closes with a simple test — content that's easy to copy is a commodity; content that's hard to copy is defensible.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Gives a concrete two-path framework (enrich AI output with proprietary data vs. differentiate elsewhere) for teams deciding how to compete once AI erases the cost advantage of writing content from scratch. Ties directly to the "human review is non-negotiable" and "differentiation" threads other experts (Natividad, Sussman) raise independently.
