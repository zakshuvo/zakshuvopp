## Content

Written in response to Google's Helpful Content Update, Indig splits search content into three buckets: contextual (gives searchers background), deep (answers a complex question), and narrative (frames reality a certain way). His claim: AI is best suited to contextual content, and he pushes back on treating "human vs. AI" as the right axis — quality should matter more than origin.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Offers a content-type taxonomy that several other experts implicitly use when arguing AI is fine for some content jobs and risky for others (e.g., Coyle's content-intelligence angle, Shepard's skepticism on deep/expert content). Useful as a shared vocabulary for the eventual playbook's "when is AI-only output actually safe" section.
