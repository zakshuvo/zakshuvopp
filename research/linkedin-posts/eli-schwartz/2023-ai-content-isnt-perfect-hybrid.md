## Content

Schwartz argues neither pure-AI nor pure-human content is the right target — the best setup is hybrid. He gives a concrete example: a company that pairs analytics data with an AI tool to auto-generate content briefs, which are then edited by a network of human writers. Commenters push back that companies often expect AI to let humans produce 3x the output at the same quality bar, which he doesn't fully rebut.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Concrete example of "AI drafts the brief, human writes/edits" as a specific division of labor — more operational than most of the "AI vs human" takes and worth cross-referencing against how Coyle (MarketMuse) and Sussman describe content-brief tooling.
