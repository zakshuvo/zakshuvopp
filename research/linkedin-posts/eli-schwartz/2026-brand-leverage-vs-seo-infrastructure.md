## Content
Schwartz argues that "brand leverage" — not raw SEO/LLM visibility — is becoming the real growth lever. He cites Aleyda Solís' SERP research comparing January 2025 to January 2026, which found AI visibility rising, organic click share falling, and paid text ads gaining ground across verticals; in high-intent categories, paid now captures up to 30% of clicks while zero-click behavior stays roughly flat. His conclusion: clicks aren't vanishing, they're concentrating into monetized placements. He frames strong SEO/LLM visibility as necessary "infrastructure" but explicitly not the same as competitive leverage — if informational queries get resolved inside AI answers while commercial intent concentrates in paid placements, organic traffic becomes a weaker proxy for business growth, and the old publish → capture → retarget → convert funnel stops scaling the way it used to.
