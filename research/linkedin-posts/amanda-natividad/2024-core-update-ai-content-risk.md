## Content

In the comments/repost thread around this post, Natividad's circle flags that Google's core update commentary is directly relevant to teams generating marketing content with AI: using automation or cheap human labor to publish low-quality content at scale is treated by Google the same way keyword stuffing was — as a tactic that eventually gets penalized, not a shortcut around real quality. The framing: high-volume, low-effort AI content is a fragile growth strategy, not a durable one.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Useful as a cautionary counterpoint in the playbook to purely "AI lets you scale content production" narratives — ties directly to Google's own stated policy against automation used to manipulate rankings, and reinforces the "differentiation over volume" theme also present in Kevin Indig's posts.
