## Content
Natividad argues that effective 2026 marketing writing requires deliberately balancing "vibes" (feelings, narrative, emotional resonance — what makes content spread) with "facts" (data, evidence, citations — what makes an idea survive scrutiny once it leaves the feed). She grounds this in behavioral science: citing Kahneman's fast/slow thinking and the affect heuristic, she argues people form impressions emotionally first and rationalize with evidence only if it's available, so responsible marketing writing needs to supply both. Her explicit target is LLM-generated writing that either drowns in unsupported "vibes" claims or lists citations without narrative — she frames the discipline of writing "like the smart human you are" as pairing a memorable claim with real, specific, recent facts, rather than defaulting to either pure data-dumping or pure assertion.

## Why this post matters for the playbook
Directly addresses a recurring practical problem for AI-assisted content teams: LLM drafts tend to either fabricate confident-sounding claims with no backing, or over-cite without a coherent narrative thread. Natividad's "vibes + facts" framing is a concrete editorial checklist item distinct from the more strategic zero-click arguments in her other collected posts — useful for the playbook's writing/editing-process section specifically.
