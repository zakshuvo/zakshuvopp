## Content

Law describes replacing a recurring manual chore — refreshing Ahrefs' data-driven articles every month (pulling fresh API data, rebuilding charts, handing off to design, updating WordPress) — with an automated pipeline. The new setup pulls fresh data on a schedule, regenerates charts/tables, drops the update into a WordPress draft, applies small copyedits like changed dates or sample sizes, and then emails the team that the article is ready for human review. He frames this as the realization of what he'd always wanted AI automation to be, rather than a chat-based writing aid.

## Why this post matters for the playbook

Concrete example of AI used for maintenance/refresh work on existing content rather than net-new drafting — a different use case than most collected material, and one that keeps a human-review gate at the end. Strong candidate for a "AI for content maintenance, not just creation" section.
