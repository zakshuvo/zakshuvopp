## Content
Law announces the beta launch of Ahrefs' AI Content Helper, positioning it explicitly against what he calls the "dark side" of most content-optimization tools: rewarding keyword-stuffing and encouraging writers to switch their brains off in favor of reductive content scores. The tool instead uses AI to identify the core topics real searchers expect for a given keyword, then scores a draft (and competitors) against topic coverage rather than keyword density. Features include picking a specific search intent from a SERP with multiple intents, viewing text snippets from top-ranking competitors for brainstorming, live-updating coverage scores as a writer edits, and AI-assisted metadata brainstorming (titles, meta descriptions). Law's stated philosophy: this approach keeps the writer "in the driving seat" with competitor data to inform their own process, instead of delegating creativity wholesale and producing another copycat article.
