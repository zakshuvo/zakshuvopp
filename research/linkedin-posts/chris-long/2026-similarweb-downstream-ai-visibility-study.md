## Content
Long summarizes a Similarweb clickstream study ("The Downstream Impact of AI Visibility") that tracked real users for seven days after seeing a ChatGPT brand recommendation, across Finance, Travel, and Beauty verticals. Key findings he highlights: (1) users who saw a brand recommended by ChatGPT were 2.5x more likely to visit that brand's site within a week; (2) ChatGPT is used disproportionately early in the buying journey (35% of usage in the "discovering" phase); (3) most AI-influenced visits get misattributed — 55% of AI-influenced users arrived at the brand's site via a subsequent search rather than a direct AI referral link, versus 40% for standard (non-AI-influenced) visits; (4) AI-influenced visitors engage roughly twice as deeply (pageviews and time-on-site) as non-AI-influenced visitors. His takeaway for practitioners: standard analytics dashboards will attribute this traffic to "organic search," hiding the AI recommendation that actually drove it — so AI visibility should be measured as a distinct input even though it won't show up cleanly in referral data.
