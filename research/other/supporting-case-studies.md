# Supporting Case Studies & Data Points

Collected alongside the expert list — third-party evidence that either
supports or complicates what the 10 experts claim. Use these to fact-check
claims before they go in the playbook, not as primary sources themselves.

## Bankrate (YMYL content, AI + human review)
- In-house AI drafting for evergreen personal-finance topics, always followed
  by human editorial review, plus a Financial Review Board for accuracy.
- Relevant to: Jeff Coyle's enterprise-scale point, Eli Schwartz's skepticism
  about unsupervised AI content in regulated niches.
- Source: https://rankai.ai/articles/ai-startup-seo-case-study-examples-rapid-growth
  (secondary source — verify against Bankrate's own statements if this goes
  into a public playbook; this is a marketing blog summarizing the case, not
  Bankrate itself)

## eesel AI (self-reported growth claim — treat with skepticism)
- Claims growth from 700 to 750,000 daily search impressions in 3 months using
  AI-generated blog posts at scale (self-published vendor case study).
- Flag: this is the vendor's own claim about their own tool — needs independent
  verification before use in any playbook. Good example of the kind of claim
  our 10 vetted experts would (and should) be skeptical of.
- Source: https://www.eesel.ai/blog/what-is-programmatic-seo

## Mid-market SaaS programmatic SEO (Arete analysis)
- 38% organic session share from ~1,400 use-case/integration pages using
  AI-assisted generation; claims AI-Overview-cited brands saw +27% branded
  search in 6 months.
- Source: https://arete.so/blog/ai-seo-saas-companies (third-party analysis
  firm — same caveat: verify methodology before citing as fact)

## General market signal
- Multiple 2026 sources converge on the same shift: content must be structured
  for both classic SEO ranking AND for citation/extraction by AI answer
  engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) — often called
  GEO/AEO. This is the throughline connecting all 10 experts' current work.
